## Grant Information

**Title:**
OppForge: AI-Driven Community Growth Engine for the Arbitrum Ecosystem

---

**Project Details:**

OppForge is a production-ready, AI-powered platform that serves as a community growth engine for the Arbitrum ecosystem by making Arbitrum's grants, bounties, and programs discoverable to the entire Web3 community. The platform aggregates opportunities from 15+ sources across 50+ chains — including Arbitrum's own STIP, LTIPP, Questbook, and Stylus programs — into a single AI-analyzed feed, then helps users apply through an AI-powered workspace that drafts tailored proposals and applications.

The community growth angle is direct: every OppForge user discovers and engages with Arbitrum ecosystem opportunities. When a bounty hunter in Nigeria finds an Arbitrum security audit on OppForge, or a community manager in Vietnam discovers an Arbitrum-funded growth role, or a hackathon dev in Brazil sees an Arbitrum Stylus Sprint — those are new Arbitrum community members who would never have found these opportunities through traditional channels.

The platform uses a sophisticated multi-agent AI architecture: a Classifier Agent built on Groq-hosted Llama 3.3 70B for opportunity categorization, a Scoring Agent with hybrid AI+ML scoring (0-100) weighted across quality (30%), skill-match (25%), reward (20%), urgency (15%), and difficulty (10%), a Risk Analysis Agent for trust verification with scam detection, and a Forge Chat Agent using RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) with ChromaDB vector store and sentence-transformer embeddings for contextual proposal generation. The system also integrates Claude and Grok model capabilities through the Forge workspace for advanced drafting assistance.

Smart contracts are deployed on Arbitrum (testnet: [ARBISCAN LINKS]) handling subscriptions, NFT membership, and on-chain reputation tracking — making Arbitrum the native payment and identity layer for the platform.

This grant funds the community expansion phase: content creation, creator bounties, educational materials, and user acquisition campaigns — all designed to onboard Web3 participants onto Arbitrum through OppForge.

---

**What innovation or value will your project bring to Arbitrum? What previously unaddressed problems is it solving?**

**Problem #1: Arbitrum's opportunities don't reach the global Web3 community.**
Arbitrum runs some of the best grant programs in Web3 (STIP, LTIPP, Questbook DDA). But discovery is fragmented — they're posted on Questbook, the Arbitrum forum, and Twitter. Talented builders in Africa, Southeast Asia, and LATAM often never see them. OppForge solves this by scraping every Arbitrum opportunity and surfacing it to a global user base alongside AI-powered scoring and application tools. This directly increases the quality and diversity of Arbitrum's grant applicant pool.

**Problem #2: No unified community growth platform exists for Arbitrum.**
Arbitrum has funded many individual community events and education programs. But there's no persistent platform that continuously onboards new community members to Arbitrum. OppForge provides this — every user who subscribes (on Arbitrum), every Founder NFT minted, and every mission completed is a new Arbitrum community member with on-chain history.

**Problem #3: Web3 participants can't evaluate opportunity trustworthiness.**
Testnet farmers, bounty hunters, and airdrop seekers regularly interact with scam protocols. OppForge's Risk AI Agent — a novel, multi-factor trust scoring system analyzing scam patterns (30%), source reliability (25%), legitimacy (20%), URL safety (15%), and reward realism (10%) — protects users before they sign transactions. This is a genuinely new mechanism that no other Arbitrum-adjacent platform offers.

**Innovation:** OppForge introduces a "Meta-Community-Growth" model — a platform that grows Arbitrum's community automatically by being useful to the broader Web3 ecosystem. It's not a one-off event. It's persistent infrastructure that compounds over time.
